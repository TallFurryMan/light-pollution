# Student Path

## Your mission

- Place a light sensor that has already been prepared by the teacher.
- Compare a dark location with a brighter one.
- Read the map and the lux values in Home Assistant.
- Explain what the measurements say about light pollution.

## What you do not need to do

- Reflash the node.
- Change radio settings.
- Troubleshoot the gateway.

Those steps are prepared in advance so the activity stays focused on science and observation.

## Classroom flow

1. Check the name of the kit assigned to your group.
2. Install the sensor in the chosen place.
3. Write down what is nearby: streetlight, car park, school yard, windows, trees, road.
4. Wait for the measurement to appear on the map.
5. Compare your point with another group.
6. Write a short conclusion: location, measured value, and your explanation.

## What the system shows

- A point on the map.
- A light measurement.
- A history view to see whether the light stays stable or changes over time.

## Questions to discuss

- Why can two nearby places have very different values?
- What changes when lights turn on or off?
- Which living things are disturbed by too much light at night?
- How could we reduce light without making a place unsafe?

## Safety

- Keep the sensor dry while handling it.
- Do not pull on the wires or connectors.
- Never short the battery.
- Tell the teacher if a box gets hot, smells odd, or looks damaged.
